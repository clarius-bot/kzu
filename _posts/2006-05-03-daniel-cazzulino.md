#####  [MVP Summit: simply awesome!](http://clariusconsulting.net/blogs/kzu/archive/2004/04/13/200.aspx)

Last week we had the [2004 MVP Global Summit](http://mvp.support.microsoft.com/default.aspx?scid=fh;en-us;mvpsmt2004), as you probably read from [Dare Obasanjo](http://www.25hoursaday.com/weblog/PermaLink.aspx?guid=b6ec9bc8-cdb7-410b-8ea1-06c5e92538ee) post. I met most of the XML MVP guys, and we spend a good time together, discussing technologies, the future of .NET and APIs, etc.   
Despite the usual marketing stuff (the first 2 days), the third day and the non-conference-related activities made for an awesome experience. We had the chance to talk with the guys developing the cool System.Xml stuff, about the current and the future versions, as well as their view on some other aspects of the XML landscape and its evolution.

I was amazed by the degree of openness in these guys. We really felt like being part of the same team, the team pushing for XML adoption and struggling to get the best XML API in the world. It was really encouraging. A big thanks for them, and congratulations for a really cool Summit!
