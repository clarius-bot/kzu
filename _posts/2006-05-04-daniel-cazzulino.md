## Friday, April 21, 2006 - Posts

#####  [MSDN Architecture Webcast: Extending Microsoft patterns & practices ObjectBuilder](http://clariusconsulting.net/blogs/kzu/archive/2006/04/21/MsdnOBWebcast.aspx)

Today I presented a [webcast on p&p ObjectBuilder](http://msevents.microsoft.com/cui/WebCastEventDetails.aspx?culture=en-us&EventID=1032293738&CountryCode=us "MSDN Architecture Webcast: Extending Microsoft patterns & practices ObjectBuilder \(Level 200\)") (OB), the underpinning framework powering [CAB](http://msdn.microsoft.com/practices/apptype/smartclient/default.aspx?pull=/library/en-us/dnpag2/html/cab.asp "Smart Client - Composite UI Application Block at MSDN") and [EntLib v2.0](http://msdn.microsoft.com/library/?url=/library/en-us/dnpag2/html/EntLib2.asp "Enterprise Library for .NET Framework 2.0").

I'm afraid I tried to cover too much ground and the result was a pretty fast and maybe hard to follow session. I think it would have been much better to do an OB introduction webcast alone, and then another one on how to extend it. My apologies for that. I'll appreciate your feedback. 

I showed some examples of how to use OB programmatically, as well as how to extend it. You can [download the source](http://clariusconsulting.net/downloads/farcaster.zip "Sample code showcasing OB, Spring and Pico together"), which is fairly big as it contains [Pico.NET](http://www.picocontainer.org/ "PicoContainer site") and [Spring.NET](http://www.springframework.org/ "SpringFramework homepage") in addition to OB itself. Also, you will see a side-by-side example of [Fowler's MovieFinder example](http://www.martinfowler.com/articles/injection.html "Martin Fowler on Inversion of Control Containers and the Dependency Injection pattern") running in all three frameworks, so you can appreciate the differences.
